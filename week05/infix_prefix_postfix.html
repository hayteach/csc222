Infix, Prefix, and Postfix

Notations for arithmetic expressions and their relation to stacks

Overview

Infix places the operator between operands (a + b). Prefix (Polish notation) places the operator before operands (+ a b). Postfix (Reverse Polish Notation) places the operator after operands (a b +).

Why Postfix?

Many compilers convert arithmetic expressions to postfix because it can be evaluated easily using a stack.
