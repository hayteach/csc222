COURSE DESCRIPTION This course will explore the application of software engineering techniques, data structures, and associated algorithms to the design and development of computer programs. Programs are written in an appropriate and modern object-oriented language.(C-ID: COMP-132) Course Objectives/Learning Outcomes: Course Objectives: Write programs that use each of the following data structures: arrays, records, strings, linked-lists, stacks, queues and hash tables. Implement, test and debug simple recursive functions and procedures. Evaluate tradeoffs in lifetime management (reference counting vs garbage collection) Explain how abstraction mechanisms support the creation of reusable software components. Design, implement, test, and debug simple programs in an object-oriented programming language. Compare and contrast object-oriented analysis and design with structured analysis and design. Learning Outcomes Upon successful completion of the course the student should be able to: Write programs that implement and use each of the following data structures: arrays, records, strings, linked lists, stacks, queues, and hash tables. Compare iterative and recursive solutions for elementary problems such as factorial. Compare and contrast object-oriented analysis and design with structured analysis and design. Discuss the representation and use of primitive data types and built-in data structures. Choose the appropriate data structure for modeling a given problem. Identify and describe the properties of a variable such as its associated address, value, scope, persistence, and size. Describe how the class mechanism supports encapsulation and information hiding. ABOUT ME Hi, My name is Heidi Ahders and I will be your instructor for this course. I am originally from the San Francisco East Bay. I graduated from Acalanes High School in Lafayette. After I graduated from high school, I attended Diablo Valley College where I completed my general education and then moved on to Sonoma State University where I met my husband, Roger Ahders. I complete my BA in Mathematics there in 1991.  My plan was to become a math teacher either in a junior high or high school. I completed my teaching credential there at Sonoma State in 1992 and began my teaching career as a substitute teacher in Sonoma County.  My husband then was accepted to Oregon State University so we moved to Corvallis, OR where I continued to be a substitute teacher. In 1993 we had our son and which time I took some time off from teaching to help my family's business where I could take my son to work and spend time with him. I eventually went back to teaching and at the same time decided to pursue a master's degree in math education. After teaching for a year, I started my path in the computer field. I was given an opportunity to intern at HP in the Inkjet Division. This gave me the opportunity to create webpages. This led to contract job within HP for the Mobile Computing Division, where I created a web application. I was also working part-time at the School District in the Technology Department where I created a district-wide application.  In 1998, Roger was hired at Mendocino College and I was hired on his coat tails as a part-time math instructor.  We then had our daughter in 1999 and I didn't want to leave her so I started teaching online and at night. I loved working on computers, specifically programming, web and application development, so decided to move to teaching in the computer science department where I could teach online. I started working on a Master's in Computer Science at Georgia Institute of Technology and in 2018 received my degree.  I hope that I learn more from you than you learn from me and that everyone has a great semester.
